01  /  08

~7 min drive

Walt Disney World Resort

Just a short hop away, Walt Disney World is home to four iconic theme parks — Magic Kingdom, EPCOT, Hollywood Studios, and Animal Kingdom — plus two water parks and the free-to-enter Disney Springs shopping and dining district. You could spend an entire week here and still not see everything. For investors, this is the single most powerful driver of occupancy in the STR market.

02  /  08

~35 min drive

Universal Orlando Resort

Home to The Wizarding World of Harry Potter and the brand-new Epic Universe — Universal delivers some of the most thrilling and immersive rides on the planet. A must for families, teens, and anyone who wants a little more edge in their theme park experience. Epic Universe's opening has further strengthened Orlando's position as the world's premier tourism destination.

03  /  08

~28 min drive

SeaWorld Orlando

A perfect blend of heart-pumping roller coasters and up-close animal encounters. SeaWorld's conservation-focused atmosphere makes it equally exciting and educational, with standout shows, aquatic exhibits, and family-friendly thrills for all ages. A full day out that offers something completely different from the Disney and Universal experience.

04  /  08

~22 min drive

Old Town Kissimmee

A free-to-enter Americana-style entertainment district right on US-192. Carnival rides, go-karts, shops, live music, and the famous Saturday Night Classic Car Cruise. A laid-back, family-friendly evening that won't cost a park ticket — and a perfect option for those mid-trip nights when guests want something low-key close to home.

Greater Orlando

74 million visitors a year. The world's greatest theme parks. And Lighthouse Key right at the centre of it all.

05  /  08

~22 min drive

Disney Springs

No park ticket required. Disney Springs is a sprawling waterfront district packed with over 100 shops, restaurants, and entertainment options — from live music to bowling to a soaring hot air balloon ride. A perfect evening out without the theme park crowds, and a destination guests return to multiple times per stay.

06  /  08

~5 min drive

Island H2O Water Park

One of the closest attractions to the resort. This social media–themed water park features an exciting mix of high-speed slides, a lazy river, wave pool, and cabana rentals. A great way to cool off on a hot Florida afternoon without going far — and a compelling reason for families to choose Lighthouse Key over properties further from the action.

07  /  08

~20 min drive

Medieval Times Dinner & Tournament

An unforgettable dinner show where guests cheer on jousting knights while feasting on a four-course meal — no silverware required. Equal parts spectacle and delicious chaos, it's a guaranteed hit with kids and adults alike. The kind of experience guests remember long after the trip ends, and tell everyone about when they get home.

08  /  08

~40 min drive

LEGOLAND Florida Resort

A fantastic option for families with younger children. LEGOLAND is built around creativity and hands-on fun, with rides, a water park, and a full SEA LIFE Aquarium — all with shorter lines and a more relaxed pace than the mega-parks. Worth the day trip for the right demographic, and another reason families extend their Orlando stay by one more night.
