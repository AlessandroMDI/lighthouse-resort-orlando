A rising tide that lifts every property on the corridor.

Universal's Epic Universe — the first major new theme park in the US in 25 years — opened May 2025 and is already reshaping Orlando's tourism landscape. Short-term rental bookings across the corridor are up 16% since opening. Universal's own president confirmed the park is "driving longer stays, higher per cap spending, and increased demand across hotels." Lighthouse Key is positioned to capture that expanded demand base from day one of stabilisation.

SeaWorld Orlando adds a distinct day-trip option to every guest itinerary — roller coasters, marine encounters, and an atmosphere entirely different from Disney and Universal. The water park that's practically on-site.

At 5 minutes, Island H2O is the closest major water attraction to Lighthouse Key on the entire corridor. Slides for all ages, a lazy river, wave pool, and water features — a full family day out that requires almost no logistics.

For investors, ultra-proximate amenities of this quality reduce the distance gap that might otherwise drive a guest to a competitor property further along the highway.

Atlantic or Gulf. Either way, it's worth the drive.

Cocoa Beach, 75 minutes east — Atlantic waves, a classic surf town, and a natural add-on to a Kennedy Space Center visit on the same day.
